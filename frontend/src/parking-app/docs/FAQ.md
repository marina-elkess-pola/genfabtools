# GenFabTools Parking Engine — Frequently Asked Questions

---

## 1. Is this code-compliant?

**No.** This tool uses generic U.S. parking standards for estimation purposes only.

It does not reference your local zoning code, ADA Accessibility Guidelines for your specific building type, or state-specific requirements. The stall counts and dimensions shown are representative values to support early feasibility analysis.

Before finalizing any parking design, consult with a licensed professional who can apply the specific codes and standards that govern your project.

---

## 2. Can I use this for permits?

**No.** The output from this tool is not suitable for permit applications.

Permit drawings require precise dimensions, code-compliant layouts, accessible route planning, and professional certification. This tool provides conceptual estimates to help you evaluate options before you reach that stage.

Think of this as a calculator for planning conversations — not a replacement for engineered drawings.

---

## 3. Why did I lose stalls compared to what I expected?

Several factors can reduce the number of stalls that fit:

- **Aisle width:** Two-way aisles require 24 feet of width, which consumes more area than one-way aisles (typically 12–15 feet).

- **Setback:** The setback distance you enter removes area from the perimeter of the site before stalls are placed.

- **Site shape:** Narrow or irregular sites have more unusable edge area. A perfectly rectangular lot is most efficient.

- **Geometry loss:** Some area at the ends of rows cannot fit a complete stall and is left empty.

Check the "Why not more stalls?" hint in the results panel — it shows the primary factor limiting your stall count.

---

## 4. Why doesn't structured parking work yet?

Structured (multi-level) parking involves significantly more complexity than surface lots:

- Ramp placement and circulation
- Column grids that constrain stall layout
- Vertical cores for stairs and elevators
- Floor-to-floor height calculations

We chose to release surface parking first so you can start getting value from the tool now. Structured parking is in development and will be available in a future update.

The "Structured" option is visible but disabled, marked as "Coming Soon."

---

## 5. Who is this tool for?

This tool is designed for professionals involved in early-stage site planning:

- **Developers** evaluating whether a site can meet parking requirements
- **Architects** estimating feasibility before detailed design
- **Planners** comparing parking capacity across multiple sites
- **Consultants** providing quick assessments during client meetings

It is not intended for:

- Final design or construction documentation
- Code compliance verification
- Permit submission

If you're asking "Can this site support the parking I need?" — this tool can help you answer that question in minutes instead of hours.
